About

A quick look at my background, research path, and what drives my work in AI.

Academic Profile

My path into AI started from a simple question: how do systems decide, and how can we make those decisions understandable? Over time, this grew into a research vision combining logic, simulation, and interaction to study how intelligent systems reason in complex environments.

I am a PhD student within the National PhD in AI - Agrifood & Environment at the University of Naples Federico II. I work at the intersection of artificial intelligence, formal methods, and digital twin technologies, aiming to build AI that is both capable and explainable.

Artificial Intelligence Formal Methods Digital Twins Neurosymbolic AI

Current Research

I contribute to the European project INFANT, developing intelligent and adaptive frameworks for pediatric healthcare and education. I design interactive simulations and reasoning modules that model adaptive behavior and social interaction, combining temporal logics with data-driven learning.

I am also developing an AI for videogames that uses temporal logic to reason about time, goals, and strategy, giving agents more realistic behavior in dynamic scenarios.

Beyond Research

I care about clear ideas and useful tools. Outside the lab, I build small prototypes and game environments to test how formal reasoning can make AI more expressive and fun to interact with. I see creativity as a practical part of good research, it keeps logic grounded in experience.

I regularly join conferences and workshops (e.g., AAMAS, EUMAS) on multi-agent systems, human-AI interaction, and logic-based AI, which continuously shape how I approach transparent, human-aligned intelligent systems.
