I completed my studies in Computer Engineering at the University of Naples Federico II, where I first approached the world of computational logic, agent systems, and intelligent simulations. Over time, I became increasingly fascinated by the idea of combining formal logics with machine learning to create systems capable not only of making predictions, but also of explaining and justifying their choices. This interest naturally evolved into the field of neurosymbolic AI, which now forms the backbone of my research.

Research & Projects

I currently work within the European project INFANT, which aims to build an integrated pediatric oncology network in Campania through the development of intelligent digital infrastructures and decision-support systems. My role focuses on the design of interactive simulations and reasoning modules to model adaptive behaviors and clinical pathways, leveraging temporal logics (ATL/CTL) and symbolic representations integrated with data-driven models.

Beyond INFANT, I have been involved in the design of digital twin architectures for research and education, and in the development of AI-driven simulations in Unreal Engine â€” environments that merge formal verification and strategic reasoning to study agent behavior in dynamic contexts. Much of my work revolves around the question of how to make AI systems both interpretable and interactive, allowing humans to understand and influence the reasoning process behind complex decisions.

Academic Activities

My academic path has been deeply shaped by the interaction with the AI research community. Over the years, I have taken part in seminars, demo sessions, and interdisciplinary workshops, presenting my work and exchanging ideas with researchers from different backgrounds. These experiences have strengthened my vision of AI as a discipline that blends mathematical precision with creative problem-solving, where logic, simulation, and human-centered design coexist. I am especially passionate about fostering dialogue between symbolic reasoning and machine learning, aiming to develop technologies that are not only powerful, but also transparent, inclusive, and aligned with human understanding.
