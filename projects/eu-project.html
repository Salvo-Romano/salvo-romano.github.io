Project Overview

The INFANT project aims to create a cutting-edge pediatric oncology network across Campania, Italy, combining advanced artificial intelligence, data analytics, and Internet of Things (IoT) technologies. The initiative focuses on improving diagnostic processes, treatment personalization, and patient monitoring by leveraging AI-powered decision support systems and real-time data from various healthcare devices.

Objectives

  • Develop AI-driven systems to enhance clinical decision-making in pediatric oncology.
  • Create digital twins of pediatric cancer patients to simulate treatment pathways and outcomes.
  • Build an interoperable data platform for collecting and analyzing real-time health data, enabling personalized treatment.
  • Design user-friendly interfaces for clinicians, researchers, and caregivers to easily interpret the results and adapt care plans.

My Role in INFANT

As a PhD researcher in Work Package 3, my role focuses on designing and implementing intelligent systems that can model and analyze complex medical data. Specifically, I work on developing cognitive reasoning modules using temporal logics (ATL/CTL) to support clinical decision-making, as well as creating interactive simulations for personalized treatment modeling. My contributions also involve integrating real-time patient data with AI models, ensuring that the systems are both clinically relevant and user-friendly.

Key Outcomes and Deliverables

  • Design of an interactive clinical decision-support system based on temporal logics and patient data models.
  • Development of a digital twin framework for simulating pediatric oncology treatment pathways.
  • Creation of a data platform to support collaboration across the INFANT network, enabling seamless integration of research and clinical workflows.
