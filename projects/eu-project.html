Project Overview

INFANT is part of Italy‚Äôs National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR) within the MUSA initiative. The project aims to create an intelligent and adaptive framework to support the regional pediatric oncology network in Campania. It integrates AI-driven reasoning, data analytics, and digital infrastructures to connect research, clinical centers, and decision-making tools for more transparent and effective healthcare processes.

Objectives

  • Develop AI reasoning models to assist clinical and organizational decisions in pediatric oncology.
  • Promote the use of formal logic and neurosymbolic AI to make healthcare systems more explainable and trustworthy.
  • Design digital tools for knowledge sharing and interoperability across hospitals and research centers.
  • Foster cross-disciplinary collaboration between AI researchers, clinicians, and data scientists.

My Role in INFANT

As a researcher at the University of Naples Federico II, I contribute to the research activities of the project, focusing on reasoning and knowledge representation within intelligent healthcare systems. My work involves exploring temporal and strategic logics (ATL/CTL) for modeling decision processes and investigating neurosymbolic approaches that combine formal reasoning with machine learning techniques. Rather than development tasks, my contribution is mainly theoretical ‚Äî designing, analyzing, and validating models that can explain and predict behaviors in complex clinical environments.
